AI Agency Business Concept — 1099 Processing MVP

Summary

Chase and a friend were discussing a Y Combinator-inspired idea: build an AI agency that replaces professional services headcount with AI agents, starting with accounting and finance. Through brainstorming, the concept crystallized into a managed service model — not SaaS — targeting midsized companies for 1099 processing. The core insight: charge service-level prices ($299–$699/season) while delivering at software-level costs.

A tax domain expert (Will Rosler) reviewed the concept and added critical domain detail: the real complexity in 1099 processing isn't the filing itself — it's getting the correct vendor list in the first place. QBO exports aren't cash-basis-aware, CC payments must be excluded, C-corps don't qualify, and the $2,000 threshold (2026, per OBBBA) applies to cash payments only. These upstream cleanup steps are where the AI earns its keep.

Plans 1–3 are now fully shipped as Sterling — a self-serve SaaS tool for accountants targeting solo CPAs and small bookkeeping firms with 50+ SMB clients. The 1099 Center is a $4/form AI-managed add-on. Plans 4–7 cover what's left to build.

Key Topics

  • AI agency as managed service → evolved to accountant-facing SaaS (Sterling)
  • 1099 processing as the initial service offering and validation wedge
  • QBO OAuth + CSV upload with provider-agnostic AccountingProvider interface
  • Client journey: Submit → Compile → Chase W-9s → Review → Clarify → File
  • Agent model: AI handles ~90% autonomously, human reviewer handles escalations
  • Three-tier SaaS pricing: FREE / STARTER $25/mo / MULTI_ENTITY $79/mo + $4/form 1099 add-on
  • Tech stack: Next.js 16, Prisma + Neon, Clerk, Stripe, Inngest, Resend
  • Vendor canonicalization as the moat — resolving name variants across QBO + bank feeds
  • Plans 4–7: production hardening → 1099 managed service → accountant platform → scale

Decisions & Insights

  • Pivot: accountant ICP over direct SMB — one accountant = 20–200 SMB clients; cleaner targeting, B2B sales motion, lower CAC
  • SaaS + managed 1099 add-on — Sterling is self-serve; 1099 Center is $4/form AI-managed
  • Vendor canonicalization is the moat — DIY filing tools expect clean data; Sterling produces it across QBO + bank feeds
  • QBO OAuth in MVP — pull raw transactions directly from API; exports aren't cash-basis-aware
  • Provider-agnostic AccountingProvider interface — QBO + CSV both implement same contract; Xero/FreshBooks added with no downstream changes
  • Inngest for job queue — durable background functions for AI pipeline steps, no persistent worker needed on Vercel
  • Plaid is V1, not V2 — without bank feeds, canonicalization only has one source; becomes "a glorified Track1099"
  • IRS e-filing manual in V1 — FIRE flat file download + manual upload; IRIS API mandatory by Dec 31, 2026
  • $2,000 threshold for 2026 — OBBBA passed; earlier $600 references in docs are wrong for tax year 2026
  • Validation signal: 5 paying companies complete a full job, 3 say they'd return next year

Domain Context

From a tax domain expert review (Will Rosler) — the real complexity of 1099 processing.

Why the vendor list is hard

QuickBooks vendor totals reporting is not cash-basis-aware and doesn't exclude CC payments automatically. Businesses often pay vendors via multiple methods (ACH for most, check or wire for some), making categorization inconsistent. The AI agent must reconcile raw payment data to produce the correct qualifying vendor list — which is the core value, not the filing itself.

  • Required for any vendor paid $2,000+ on a cash basis in the tax year (OBBBA raised threshold from $600)
  • Excluded: C-corps, payments to accounting firms, credit card payments
  • Only ACH, wire, and check payments count
  • 1099-NEC (contractors), 1099-MISC (rent, attorneys), 1099-INT (non-bank interest)

The W-9 catch-22

To determine if a vendor needs a 1099, you need their TIN and entity type (from a W-9). But you need to know they qualify first — which requires having their info. Many vendors slip through onboarding without one. Following up near the January deadline is painful and often ignored. If TINs don't match the IRS name/ID on file, filings bounce back.

The agency solves this by proactively chasing missing W-9s via automated email outreach, well before the filing deadline.

AccountingProvider Interface

Provider-agnostic — QBO is the first implementation, CSV is the fallback

QBO OAuth 2.0

Pulls raw Purchase + BillPayment transactions directly from QBO API. Cash-basis filter and CC exclusion applied server-side.

Scope: com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting

CSV Upload

Manual fallback. Columns: vendor name, amount, payment method, entity type, EIN, date. Works for any accounting tool.

Future: Xero, FreshBooks, Wave — same interface, no downstream changes.
